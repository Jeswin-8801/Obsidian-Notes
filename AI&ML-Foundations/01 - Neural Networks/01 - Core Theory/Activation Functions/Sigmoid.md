### 1. Definition
The Sigmoid function (Logistic function) is defined as:
$$\large \sigma(x) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x}} = (1 + e^{-x})^{-1}$$


---

### 2. Derivation (Chain Rule)
To find $\sigma'(x)$, we apply the power rule and the chain rule:

Let $u = 1 + e^{-x}$. Then $\sigma(x) = u^{-1}$.

$$\frac{d\sigma}{dx} = \frac{d}{du}(u^{-1}) \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

Step-by-step:
1.  **Power Rule:** $\frac{d}{du}(u^{-1}) = -u^{-2} = -(1 + e^{-x})^{-2}$
2.  **Inner Derivative:** $\frac{d}{dx}(1 + e^{-x}) = -e^{-x}$

Combine the terms:
$$\sigma'(x) = -(1 + e^{-x})^{-2} \cdot (-e^{-x})$$
$$\sigma'(x) = \frac{e^{-x}}{(1 + e^{-x})^2}$$

---

### 3. Expressing $\sigma'(x)$ in terms of $\sigma(x)$
To simplify the expression for use in backpropagation, we manipulate the numerator:

--- start-multi-column: Algebraic Trick
number of columns: 2

**Method: Add and Subtract 1**
$$\sigma'(x) = \frac{1 + e^{-x} - 1}{(1 + e^{-x})^2}$$

Split the fraction:
$$\sigma'(x) = \frac{1 + e^{-x}}{(1 + e^{-x})^2} - \frac{1}{(1 + e^{-x})^2}$$

--- end-column ---

**Simplification**
$$\sigma'(x) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x}} - \left( \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x}} \right)^2$$

Since $\sigma(x) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x}}$:
$$\large \sigma'(x) = \sigma(x) - \sigma(x)^2$$

--- end-multi-column

> [!abstract] Final Closed Form
> $$\large \frac{d}{dx} \sigma(x) = \sigma(x) \left( 1 - \sigma(x) \right)$$

---

### 4. Properties for Deep Learning
* **Range:** The derivative is bounded between $[0, 0.25]$.
* **Peak:** The maximum gradient occurs at $x = 0$, where $\sigma'(0) = 0.5(1 - 0.5) = 0.25$.
* **Vanishing Gradient:** When $\sigma(x)$ approaches $0$ or $1$ (saturation), the derivative $\sigma'(x)$ approaches $0$. This causes the weights to stop updating during gradient descent.
