## Derivative of the Tanh Activation Function

### 1. Definition
The Hyperbolic Tangent (Tanh) function is defined in terms of exponential functions:
$$\large \tanh(x) = \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{e^x + e^{-x}}$$

It is related to the Sigmoid function by the identity: $\tanh(x) = 2\sigma(2x) - 1$.



---

### 2. Derivation (Quotient Rule)
To find the derivative $\frac{d}{dx}\tanh(x)$, we use the quotient rule:
$$\left( \frac{u}{v} \right)' = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$$

Let $u = e^x - e^{-x}$ and $v = e^x + e^{-x}$.

**Step-by-step Derivatives:**
1. $u' = \frac{d}{dx}(e^x - e^{-x}) = e^x + e^{-x}$ (Note: $u' = v$)
2. $v' = \frac{d}{dx}(e^x + e^{-x}) = e^x - e^{-x}$ (Note: $v' = u$)

**Substitute into the Quotient Rule:**
$$\frac{d}{dx}\tanh(x) = \frac{(e^x + e^{-x})(e^x + e^{-x}) - (e^x - e^{-x})(e^x - e^{-x})}{(e^x + e^{-x})^2}$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}\tanh(x) = \frac{(e^x + e^{-x})^2 - (e^x - e^{-x})^2}{(e^x + e^{-x})^2}$$

---

### 3. Algebraic Simplification
We can split the fraction to express the derivative in terms of $\tanh(x)$:

--- start-multi-column: Simplification
number of columns: 2

**Method: Distribute Fraction**
$$\frac{d}{dx}\tanh(x) = \frac{(e^x + e^{-x})^2}{(e^x + e^{-x})^2} - \frac{(e^x - e^{-x})^2}{(e^x + e^{-x})^2}$$

--- end-column ---

**Identity Substitution**
$$\frac{d}{dx}\tanh(x) = 1 - \left( \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{e^x + e^{-x}} \right)^2$$

Since $\tanh(x) = \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{e^x + e^{-x}}$:
$$\large \frac{d}{dx}\tanh(x) = 1 - \tanh^2(x)$$

--- end-multi-column

> [!abstract] Final Closed Form
> $$\large \frac{d}{dx} \tanh(x) = 1 - \tanh^2(x)$$

---

### 4. Comparison with Sigmoid
* **Range:** Tanh maps to $(-1, 1)$, while Sigmoid maps to $(0, 1)$.
* **Zero-Centered:** Tanh is zero-centered, which often leads to faster convergence in DNNs as gradients can be both positive and negative.
* **Peak Gradient:** The maximum gradient of Tanh is $1.0$ (at $x=0$), whereas Sigmoid’s maximum is only $0.25$. This makes Tanh less susceptible to vanishing gradients than Sigmoid, though it still saturates.
