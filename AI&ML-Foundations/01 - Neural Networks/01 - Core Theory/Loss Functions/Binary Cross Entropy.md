### 1. Definition
For a single training example with true label $y \in \{0, 1\}$ and predicted probability $\hat{y} \in [0, 1]$:
$$\large L = -\left[ y \log(\hat{y}) + (1 - y) \log(1 - \hat{y}) \right]$$

The total loss for $n$ examples is the average:
$$J = -\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left[ y_i \log(\hat{y}_i) + (1 - y_i) \log(1 - \hat{y}_i) \right]$$



---

### 2. Derivation of the Gradient
To update weights via backpropagation, we find the derivative of the loss $L$ with respect to the prediction $\hat{y}$.

$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{y}} = -\frac{\partial}{\partial \hat{y}} [y \log(\hat{y}) + (1 - y) \log(1 - \hat{y})]$$

**Step-by-step:**
1. **Derivative of $y \log(\hat{y})$:** $\frac{y}{\hat{y}}$
2. **Derivative of $(1 - y) \log(1 - \hat{y})$:** $\frac{1 - y}{1 - \hat{y}} \cdot (-1)$ (by chain rule)

**Combine the terms:**
$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{y}} = -\left[ \frac{y}{\hat{y}} - \frac{1 - y}{1 - \hat{y}} \right]$$

Common denominator:
$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{y}} = -\left[ \frac{y(1 - \hat{y}) - \hat{y}(1 - y)}{\hat{y}(1 - \hat{y})} \right] = -\left[ \frac{y - y\hat{y} - \hat{y} + y\hat{y}}{\hat{y}(1 - \hat{y})} \right]$$

> [!abstract] Final Closed Form
> $$\large \frac{\partial L}{\partial \hat{y}} = \frac{\hat{y} - y}{\hat{y}(1 - \hat{y})}$$

---

### 3. Properties & Behavior
* **Heavy Penalty:** As the prediction $\hat{y}$ moves toward the wrong class (e.g., $y=1$ but $\hat{y} \to 0$), the loss approaches infinity.
* **Information Theory:** BCE is derived from the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between the true distribution and the predicted distribution.
* **Sigmoid Integration:** When paired with a Sigmoid activation, the $\hat{y}(1 - \hat{y})$ in the denominator cancels out with the Sigmoid derivative, preventing gradient saturation.
